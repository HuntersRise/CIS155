Why You Should Watch Andor:

  • In-depth Character Development: Get an unprecedented look at Cassian Andor's journey, adding layers of complexity and realism to his character.
  • Exciting Action Sequences: From intense space battles to on-the-ground skirmishes, the show delivers thrilling action scenes that keep you on the edge of your seat.
  • Gripping Storytelling: The narrative unfolds in unexpected ways, delving into themes of resistance, sacrifice, and survival under an oppressive regime.
  • Rich World-Building: Gain insight into the Star Wars universe's political landscape and how different factions navigate a galaxy in turmoil.
  • Unique Cinematic Style: With high-quality production values and cinematography that capture the Star Wars atmosphere, the show looks like a cinematic masterpiece.
  • Compelling Supporting Cast: A diverse array of characters each contribute depth to the story and help flesh out Cassian's world.
  • A Fresh Perspective on the Rebellion: Unlike other Star Wars content, *Andor* provides a gritty, grounded look at the early Rebellion efforts.
  • High-Stakes Plotlines: With each episode, the stakes continue to rise, keeping audiences fully engaged.
  • Darker and Mature Themes: *Andor* isn't afraid to tackle the darker side of resistance, presenting moral ambiguity and tough decisions.
  • For Fans and Newcomers Alike: While it ties into the larger Star Wars story, *Andor* offers standalone value, making it enjoyable for newcomers too.

Show Runner Information: Tony Gilroy

The showrunner for Andor, Tony Gilroy, is an acclaimed American screenwriter, director, and producer known for his exceptional storytelling and contributions to Hollywood. With an impressive career spanning multiple genres and formats, Gilroy has established himself as a master of suspenseful narratives, complex characters, and morally intricate worlds, making him the perfect fit to helm Andor, the Star Wars series that dives into the gritty underbelly of the Rebellion against the Empire.


Born in Manhattan, New York, in 1956, Gilroy grew up in a family steeped in creativity. His father, Frank D. Gilroy, was a Pulitzer Prize-winning playwright, and his brothers, Dan and John Gilroy, also ventured into film as a screenwriter and film editor, respectively. This early exposure to the arts profoundly shaped Gilroy’s approach to storytelling, blending literary depth with cinematic flair. He attended Washingtonville High School, where he developed a passion for literature and film. After high school, he moved to Hollywood and started his career as a screenwriter, writing smaller projects that gradually led to more high-profile opportunities.


Gilroy first gained widespread recognition as a writer in the early 1990s, notably for The Cutting Edge (1992) and Dolores Claiborne (1995), which showcased his knack for tension-filled storytelling. However, it was his work on the Bourne film series, starting with The Bourne Identity in 2002, that catapulted him to fame. The trilogy, starring Matt Damon as a highly trained operative on the run, redefined the spy thriller genre, and Gilroy’s screenplay became the blueprint for modern action films. His subsequent role as writer and director for Michael Clayton (2007), which received multiple Oscar nominations, further solidified his reputation. The film, starring George Clooney, is a sharp legal thriller that tackles corporate malfeasance and ethical gray areas, themes that recur throughout Gilroy’s work.


In 2016, Gilroy was brought in to co-write Rogue One: A Star Wars Story, a standalone film exploring the desperate mission to retrieve the Death Star plans. His contributions included substantial rewrites and overseeing reshoots, which helped the film become both a critical and box-office success. Gilroy’s work on Rogue One marked a significant shift in the Star Wars franchise, introducing a darker, more mature tone that resonated with audiences. His involvement with Rogue One also opened the door to Andor, as Disney and Lucasfilm saw him as the perfect choice to expand Cassian Andor’s story with his nuanced, grounded approach to world-building.


When he took on the role of showrunner for Andor, Gilroy was determined to craft a story that didn’t rely on traditional Star Wars tropes. Instead, he envisioned Andor as a character-driven, spy-thriller series set against the backdrop of political tension and rebellion. Gilroy aimed to delve into the gritty, morally complex sides of the galaxy far, far away, portraying how ordinary people navigate the oppression of a powerful Empire and make sacrifices to spark change. Under his direction, Andor explores the psychological toll of rebellion, questioning loyalty, trust, and survival in a way that elevates the series beyond a typical sci-fi narrative.

Gilroy's emphasis on realism has guided the show’s visual style and storytelling, setting it apart from other Star Wars projects. He has advocated for practical effects, grounded cinematography, and authentic set designs that emphasize the stark reality of life under the Empire’s rule. His vision has drawn comparisons to political thrillers and historical dramas, making Andor a unique entry in the Star Wars universe. In this series, Gilroy’s influence is evident in the show’s pacing, sophisticated narrative arcs, and attention to character depth, all of which have garnered critical acclaim.


Outside of his work on Star Wars, Tony Gilroy continues to be a respected figure in Hollywood, known for his dedication to telling thought-provoking, layered stories. His legacy, bolstered by Andor's success, is that of a storyteller unafraid to challenge audiences, pushing them to engage with complex moral and ethical questions within the context of thrilling, high-stakes entertainment.
